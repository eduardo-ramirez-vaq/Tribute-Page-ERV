Timeline of Alexander

356 BC: Alexander is born in Pella, Macedonia, to King Philip II and Queen Olympias.

343 BC: At age 13, Alexander begins his education under the philosopher Aristotle.

340 BC: Alexander is appointed regent of Macedonia while Philip campaigns in Byzantium.

336 BC: Philip II is assassinated; Alexander ascends to the throne of Macedonia at age 20.

334 BC: Alexander invades Asia Minor, winning the Battle of Granicus against the Persian satraps.

333 BC: He defeats Darius III at the Battle of Issus.

331 BC: He founds the city of Alexandria in Egypt and is declared a son of the god Amun at the Oracle of Siwa.

331 BC: Alexander defeats Darius III again at the Battle of Gaugamela, leading to the fall of the Persian Empire.

330 BC: He captures the Persian capital of Persepolis.

323 BC: After his death, his empire is divided among his generals, known as the Diadochi, leading to the Hellenistic period.
